Like many ambitious southern New England merchants in the first decades of the nineteenth century, Fish moved to New York, entering a partnership with his cousin Joseph Grinnell of New Bedford in 1815. Their business expanded from commission sales of whale oil to encompass a wide variety of ocean trade. In 1822 Fish & Grinnell established the Swallowtail Line of Liverpool and London packets. Fish retired in 1829 and thereafter served as president of a New York bank.
