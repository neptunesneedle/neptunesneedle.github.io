Amsterdam-Rhine Canal, North Holland Canal, and other inland canals join Het IJ here; Amsterdam’s noted canal system, which circles the Centrum (city center), is fed by the Amstel R., which flows from S. The city is cut by about 40 concentric and radial canals that are flanked by streets and crossed by 400 bridges. Because of the canals, the city is called the “Venice of the North.” Among Europe’s leading ports, Amsterdam is also one of the great commercial, intellectual, and artistic cities of Europe. ---
