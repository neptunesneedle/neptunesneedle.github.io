The neighborhood named Spuyten Duyvil lies to the north of the creek. Forms northwest corner of Manhattan just north of Baker Field, 2 miles north of George Washington Bridge. Spuyten Duyvil Creek originally flowed north of Manhattan's Marble Hill. The construction of the ship canal to the south of the neighborhood in 1895 turned Marble Hill into an island, and when the original creekbed was filled in, in 1914, Marble Hill became physically attached to the Bronx, though it remained part of the borough of Manhattan for legal purposes. "Spuyten Duyvil" literally means "Devil's Spout" in Dutch; a reference to the strong and wild currents found at that location. ---
